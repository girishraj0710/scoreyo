### 4. Nouns Detailed (102 questions)
**Key Features:**
- Countable vs uncountable (information, furniture, luggage)
- Regular and irregular plurals (child-children, man-men)
- Compound noun plurals (mother-in-law → mothers-in-law)
- Collective nouns + verb agreement (team is/are)
- Possessive forms ('s vs s' vs of-phrase)
- Noun forms of verbs/adjectives (decide→decision, happy→happiness)

**Indian Focus:**
- "Informations", "furnitures", "luggages" (uncountable plurals)
- "Cousin brother/sister" (Indian English, not international)
- "The India" vs "India" (article errors)
- Wrong possessive: "Rahul book" vs "Rahul's book"

**Unique Value:**

- Banking descriptive writing rules
- IELTS Writing penalizes "informations"

### 5. Verbs Basics (103 questions)
**Key Features:**
- State vs action verbs (know, believe, love - no continuous)
- Subject-verb agreement (he/she/it + -s/-es)
- Irregular verbs (go-went-gone, eat-ate-eaten)
- Transitive vs intransitive verbs
- Linking verbs (be, seem, appear, become)
- Modal verbs (can, will, should + base verb)
- Phrasal verbs (look up, look after, look into)

**Indian Focus:**
- "I am knowing" → "I know" (state verbs)
- "I am having a car" → "I have a car" (possession)
- "He don't" → "He doesn't" (3rd person singular)
- "She go" → "She goes" (subject-verb agreement)
- "He goed" → "He went" (irregular verbs)

**Unique Value:**
- SSC/Banking exams heavily test state verbs (5-8 marks)
- Subject-verb agreement errors (most common SSC trap)
- Hindi continuous tense influence addressed
