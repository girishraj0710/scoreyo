# Subject-Verb Agreement

**Level:** B1 | **Time:** 35 mins | **Category:** Grammar

---

# What is Subject-Verb Agreement?

Subject-Verb Agreement is the grammar rule that the verb must match (agree with) its subject in number (singular/plural) and person (1st/2nd/3rd). This is THE MOST tested grammar rule in SSC, Banking, Railways, and Error Spotting sections of all competitive exams.

In simple terms: **Singular subject = Singular verb, Plural subject = Plural verb**

However, the challenge comes with tricky subjects like:
- "One of the students" (is or are?)
- "The team" (is or are?)
- "Either Ravi or his friends" (was or were?)
- "Ten rupees" (is or are?)

In SSC CGL, SSC CHSL, and Banking exams, 80% of Error Spotting questions are Subject-Verb Agreement errors. Master this topic = 10-15 marks guaranteed.

# Core Concepts

### . Basic Agreement Rule (The Foundation)

**Rule:** Singular subject takes singular verb. Plural subject takes plural verb.

**Singular Verbs:** is, was, has, does, works, plays, writes (verb + s/es)  **Plural Verbs:** are, were, have, do, work, play, write (base form)

**Examples:**
-  CORRECT: "The student **is** studying." (singular subject + singular verb)
-  INCORRECT: "The student are studying." → WHY: 'student' is singular, needs 'is'
-  CORRECT: "The students **are** studying." (plural subject + plural verb)
-  INCORRECT: "The students is studying." → WHY: 'students' is plural, needs 'are'
-  CORRECT: "Ravi **works** at TCS." (3rd person singular + verb with -s)
-  INCORRECT: "Ravi work at TCS." → WHY: 3rd person singular needs -s ending

**Common Indian Error:**
-  "My brother study in Delhi." →  "My brother **studies** in Delhi."
-  "She go to school daily." →  "She **goes** to school daily."

---

### . Singular Subjects - The Tricky Ones

**Rule:** These words are ALWAYS singular, even if they sound plural:

**List of Singular Subjects:**
- **Indefinite Pronouns:** each, every, either, neither, one, everyone, everybody, someone, somebody, anyone, anybody, no one, nobody, nothing, everything, something
- **Subjects with "every":** every student, every book, everybody
- **Collective expressions:** each of, one of, either of, neither of

**Examples:**
-  CORRECT: "**Each** student **has** a book." (singular verb 'has')
-  INCORRECT: "Each student have a book." → WHY: 'each' is always singular
-  CORRECT: "**Everyone** **is** ready." (singular verb 'is')
-  INCORRECT: "Everyone are ready." → WHY: 'everyone' is singular despite meaning many people
-  CORRECT: "**Either** of the answers **is** correct." (singular verb 'is')
-  INCORRECT: "Either of the answers are correct." → WHY: 'either' refers to one, not both
-  CORRECT: "**Nobody** **knows** the answer." (singular verb 'knows')
-  INCORRECT: "Nobody know the answer." → WHY: 'nobody' is singular

**CRITICAL ERROR - Most Common in SSC:**
-  "Each of the students **are** present." →  "Each of the students **is** present."
-  "Every one of us **are** responsible." →  "Every one of us **is** responsible."
-  "Neither of the boys **have** come." →  "Neither of the boys **has** come."

**Memory Trick:** If the word contains "one", "body", "thing" (someone, everybody, nothing), it's singular.

---

### . "One of the + Plural Noun" - The #1 Error

**Rule:** "One of the + plural noun" takes SINGULAR verb because the subject is "one", not the plural noun.

**Formula:** One of the [plural noun] + **singular verb**

**Examples:**
-  CORRECT: "One of my friends **is** a doctor." (singular verb 'is')
-  INCORRECT: "One of my friends **are** a doctor." → WHY: Subject is 'one', not 'friends'
-  CORRECT: "One of the books **was** missing." (singular verb 'was')
-  INCORRECT: "One of the books were missing." → WHY: 'One' is singular
-  CORRECT: "One of the students **has** scored 100%." (singular verb 'has')
-  INCORRECT: "One of the students have scored 100%." → WHY: Focus on 'one', ignore 'students'

**MOST COMMON SSC/Banking ERROR:**
-  "One of the reasons **are** pollution." →  "One of the reasons **is** pollution."
-  "One of the ministers **were** absent." →  "One of the ministers **was** absent."

**How to Identify:** Cover the phrase "of the students/books/ministers". What remains? "One". One is singular, so use singular verb.

---

### . Plural Subjects

**Rule:** These words are ALWAYS plural:

**List of Plural Subjects:**
- **Plural Pronouns:** both, few, many, several, others
- **Pair words:** scissors, trousers, glasses, pants (take plural verbs)
- **Collective groups:** people, children, police

**Examples:**
-  CORRECT: "**Both** of them **are** coming." (plural verb 'are')
-  INCORRECT: "Both of them is coming." → WHY: 'both' is always plural
-  CORRECT: "**Several** students **have** passed." (plural verb 'have')
-  INCORRECT: "Several students has passed." → WHY: 'several' is plural
-  CORRECT: "My **scissors** **are** sharp." (plural verb 'are')
-  INCORRECT: "My scissors is sharp." → WHY: 'scissors' is always plural
-  CORRECT: "The **police** **are** investigating." (plural verb 'are')
-  INCORRECT: "The police is investigating." → WHY: 'police' is plural

**Special Note - "People":**
-  "Many people **are** waiting." (plural)
-  "Many people **is** waiting." → WRONG
- BUT:  "The public **is** angry." (collective singular)

---

### . Collective Nouns - When Singular? When Plural?

**Rule:** Collective nouns can be singular OR plural depending on context:
- **Singular:** When the group acts as ONE UNIT (focus on the group)
- **Plural:** When individuals in the group act SEPARATELY (focus on members)

**Common Collective Nouns:** team, family, committee, government, company, class, jury, audience, crowd, staff, army, council

**Examples - Singular (Group as Unit):**
-  "The **team** **is** winning." (team acting as one unit)
-  "The **government** **has** announced." (government as single entity)
-  "The **family** **is** moving to Mumbai." (family as one unit)
-  "The **committee** **has** decided." (committee's collective decision)

**Examples - Plural (Individual Members):**
-  "The **team** **are** arguing among themselves." (individual members disagreeing)
-  "The **family** **have** different opinions." (individual family members)
-  "The **staff** **are** arriving at different times." (individual arrivals)

**How to Decide:**
- If you can replace the noun with "it" → singular verb
- If you need "they" → plural verb

**SSC/Banking Preference:** In Indian exams, collective nouns are usually treated as SINGULAR unless clearly plural context.

**Safe Rule for Exams:**
-  "The team **is** strong." (preferred in exams)
-  "The government **has** announced." (preferred in exams)

### . Compound Subjects with "and", "or", "nor"

**Rule 1 - Compound Subject with "and":** Takes PLURAL verb (because two subjects = more than one)

**Examples:**
-  CORRECT: "Ravi **and** Priya **are** friends." (plural verb 'are')
-  INCORRECT: "Ravi and Priya is friends." → WHY: Two people = plural
-  CORRECT: "Hard work **and** dedication **lead** to success." (plural verb 'lead')
-  INCORRECT: "Hard work and dedication leads to success." → WHY: Two qualities = plural

**Exception:** If two nouns refer to the SAME person/thing, use singular verb:
-  "The poet **and** philosopher **is** here." (one person who is both poet and philosopher)
-  "Bread **and** butter **is** my breakfast." (one food item)

**Rule 2 - Compound Subject with "or" or "nor":** Verb agrees with the NEAREST subject

**Formula:** Subject 1 OR Subject 2 + verb matching Subject 2

**Examples:**
-  CORRECT: "Either Ravi **or** his friends **are** coming." (verb matches 'friends' - plural)
-  INCORRECT: "Either Ravi or his friends is coming." → WHY: Nearest subject is 'friends' (plural)
-  CORRECT: "Either the students **or** the teacher **is** wrong." (verb matches 'teacher' - singular)
-  INCORRECT: "Either the students or the teacher are wrong." → WHY: Nearest subject is 'teacher' (singular)
-  CORRECT: "Neither the manager **nor** the employees **were** happy." (verb matches 'employees' - plural)
-  INCORRECT: "Neither the manager nor the employees was happy." → WHY: Match with 'employees'

**Memory Trick:** The verb "follows" the closer subject. Place the plural subject closer to the verb for easier writing.

**Better Writing:**
- Instead of: "Either the teacher or the students are..."
- Write: "Either the students or the teacher is..." (avoids confusion)

### . Quantifiers - Some, Most, All, None, Majority

**Rule:** These quantifiers can be singular OR plural depending on the noun they refer to:

**Formula:** Quantifier + of + noun → verb matches the NOUN

**Examples with Countable Nouns (Plural):**
-  CORRECT: "**Some** of the students **are** absent." (students = countable, plural)
-  INCORRECT: "Some of the students is absent." → WHY: 'students' is plural
-  CORRECT: "**Most** of the books **were** sold." (books = countable, plural)
-  INCORRECT: "Most of the books was sold." → WHY: 'books' is plural
-  CORRECT: "**All** of the employees **have** left." (employees = countable, plural)
-  INCORRECT: "All of the employees has left." → WHY: 'employees' is plural

**Examples with Uncountable Nouns (Singular):**
-  CORRECT: "**Some** of the water **is** polluted." (water = uncountable, singular)
-  INCORRECT: "Some of the water are polluted." → WHY: 'water' is uncountable
-  CORRECT: "**Most** of the information **was** correct." (information = uncountable, singular)
-  INCORRECT: "Most of the information were correct." → WHY: 'information' is singular
-  CORRECT: "**None** of the milk **has** spoiled." (milk = uncountable, singular)
-  INCORRECT: "None of the milk have spoiled." → WHY: 'milk' is singular

**Special Case - "None":**
- "None" can be singular OR plural depending on context:
-  "None of the students **is** here." (formal, emphasizing not one)
-  "None of the students **are** here." (informal, common usage)
- **Exam Tip:** In SSC/Banking, "none" is usually treated as SINGULAR for formal writing.

**Other Quantifiers:**
- **The majority of, a lot of, plenty of, the rest of** → follow the same rule (match the noun)

### . Distance, Time, Money, Weight - Always Singular

**Rule:** When distance, time, money, or weight is mentioned as a SINGLE UNIT or TOTAL AMOUNT, use SINGULAR verb.

**Examples - Distance:**
-  CORRECT: "Ten kilometers **is** a long distance." (singular verb 'is')
-  INCORRECT: "Ten kilometers are a long distance." → WHY: 10 km as one total distance
-  CORRECT: "Five miles **is** not far." (singular verb 'is')
-  INCORRECT: "Five miles are not far." → WHY: Total distance as one unit

**Examples - Time:**
-  CORRECT: "Two hours **is** enough time." (singular verb 'is')
-  INCORRECT: "Two hours are enough time." → WHY: 2 hours as one duration
-  CORRECT: "Three years **has** passed." (singular verb 'has')
-  INCORRECT: "Three years have passed." → WHY: Total time period as one unit

**Examples - Money:**
-  CORRECT: "Ten rupees **is** too much." (singular verb 'is')
-  INCORRECT: "Ten rupees are too much." → WHY: ₹10 as one amount
-  CORRECT: "Five thousand dollars **was** spent." (singular verb 'was')
-  INCORRECT: "Five thousand dollars were spent." → WHY: Total money as one sum

**Examples - Weight:**
-  CORRECT: "Ten kilograms **is** heavy." (singular verb 'is')
-  INCORRECT: "Ten kilograms are heavy." → WHY: 10 kg as one weight

**Memory Trick:** If you can say "This amount of [money/time/distance] is...", use singular verb.

**Common SSC Error:**
-  "Twenty rupees **are** not sufficient." →  "Twenty rupees **is** not sufficient."
-  "Five years **have** passed since 2015." →  "Five years **has** passed since 2015."

---

### . Subjects with Prepositional Phrases - Ignore the Middle Words

**Rule:** The verb must agree with the MAIN SUBJECT, NOT the object of the preposition. Ignore words between subject and verb.

**Common Prepositions:** of, in, on, with, along with, together with, as well as, besides, except, including

**Formula:** Subject + [prepositional phrase] + verb matching SUBJECT

**Examples:**
-  CORRECT: "The box **of chocolates** **is** on the table." (subject: box - singular)
-  INCORRECT: "The box of chocolates are on the table." → WHY: Subject is 'box', not 'chocolates'
-  CORRECT: "The students **in the classroom** **are** studying." (subject: students - plural)
-  INCORRECT: "The students in the classroom is studying." → WHY: Subject is 'students'
-  CORRECT: "The manager, **along with his team**, **is** attending." (subject: manager - singular)
-  INCORRECT: "The manager, along with his team, are attending." → WHY: 'along with' doesn't change subject

**More Examples:**
-  "The price **of mangoes** **has** increased." (subject: price - singular)
-  "The houses **in the village** **were** damaged." (subject: houses - plural)
-  "The girl **with the red dress** **is** my sister." (subject: girl - singular)
-  "The teacher, **as well as the students**, **is** excited." (subject: teacher - singular)

**How to Find Subject:** Remove everything between subject and verb. What remains?
- "The box ~~of chocolates~~ **is** on the table." → Subject: box (singular) → Use 'is'

**Common SSC Error:**
-  "The list of candidates **are** ready." →  "The list of candidates **is** ready." (subject: list)
-  "The group of boys **were** playing." →  "The group of boys **was** playing." (subject: group)

---

### . Inverted Sentence Structure - Verb Before Subject

**Rule:** In questions or sentences starting with "There/Here", the verb comes BEFORE the subject. Identify the subject first, then match the verb.

**Examples with "There":**
-  CORRECT: "There **is** a book on the table." (subject: a book - singular)
-  INCORRECT: "There are a book on the table." → WHY: Subject is 'a book' (singular)
-  CORRECT: "There **are** three students outside." (subject: three students - plural)
-  INCORRECT: "There is three students outside." → WHY: Subject is 'three students' (plural)
-  CORRECT: "There **has** been a problem." (subject: a problem - singular)
-  INCORRECT: "There have been a problem." → WHY: Subject is 'a problem' (singular)

**Examples with "Here":**
-  CORRECT: "Here **is** your pen." (subject: your pen - singular)
-  INCORRECT: "Here are your pen." → WHY: Subject is 'pen' (singular)
-  CORRECT: "Here **are** the keys." (subject: the keys - plural)
-  INCORRECT: "Here is the keys." → WHY: Subject is 'keys' (plural)

**Examples - Questions:**
-  CORRECT: "**Does** Ravi **work** here?" (subject: Ravi - singular)
-  INCORRECT: "Do Ravi work here?" → WHY: 'Ravi' is singular (3rd person)
-  CORRECT: "**Where** are the students?" (subject: students - plural)
-  INCORRECT: "Where is the students?" → WHY: 'students' is plural

**How to Check:** Rearrange the sentence to normal order (Subject + Verb):
- "There are books" → "Books are there"  Correct
- "There is books" → "Books is there"  Wrong

---

### . Titles, Names, Plural-Looking Singular Nouns

**Rule:** Titles of books/movies/organizations, proper names, and certain plural-looking words take SINGULAR verbs.

**Examples - Titles:**
-  CORRECT: "**The United States** **is** a powerful country." (singular verb 'is')
-  INCORRECT: "The United States are a powerful country." → WHY: Country name, treated as one
-  CORRECT: "**The New York Times** **publishes** daily." (singular verb 'publishes')
-  INCORRECT: "The New York Times publish daily." → WHY: Newspaper name (one entity)
-  CORRECT: "**'War and Peace'** **is** a famous novel." (singular verb 'is')
-  INCORRECT: "'War and Peace' are a famous novel." → WHY: One book title

**Examples - Plural-Looking Singular Nouns:**
-  CORRECT: "**Mathematics** **is** difficult." (singular verb 'is')
-  INCORRECT: "Mathematics are difficult." → WHY: One subject
-  CORRECT: "**Physics** **requires** practice." (singular verb 'requires')
-  INCORRECT: "Physics require practice." → WHY: One subject
-  CORRECT: "**The news** **was** shocking." (singular verb 'was')
-  INCORRECT: "The news were shocking." → WHY: 'news' is uncountable, singular

**Other Singular Nouns:** economics, politics, ethics, measles, mumps, innings

**Examples - Organizations:**
-  "**Infosys** **is** hiring." (company name - singular)
-  "**The Reserve Bank of India** **has** announced." (institution - singular)

# Common Traps in SSC/Banking Exams

## Trap 1: "One of the + Plural Noun"
-  "One of my friends **are** coming." →  "One of my friends **is** coming."
- **Why:** Subject is "one", not "friends". Always singular verb.

## Trap 2: Indefinite Pronouns (each, every, everyone, etc.)
-  "Each of the students **have** a book." →  "Each of the students **has** a book."
-  "Everyone **are** ready." →  "Everyone **is** ready."
- **Why:** All indefinite pronouns with -one, -body, -thing are singular.

## Trap 3: Subjects with Prepositional Phrases
-  "The price of mangoes **have** increased." →  "The price of mangoes **has** increased."
- **Why:** Subject is "price" (singular), not "mangoes". Ignore "of mangoes".

## Trap 4: Either/Neither + "or"
-  "Either the teacher or the students **is** wrong." →  "Either the teacher or the students **are** wrong."
- **Why:** Verb matches the NEAREST subject ("students" = plural).

## Trap 5: Distance/Time/Money as Total Amount
-  "Ten rupees **are** sufficient." →  "Ten rupees **is** sufficient."
-  "Five years **have** passed." →  "Five years **has** passed."
- **Why:** Total amount = singular unit.

## Trap 6: "There is/are"
-  "There **is** many students." →  "There **are** many students."
- **Why:** Verb matches the subject AFTER "there" (students = plural).

## Trap 7: Collective Nouns
-  "The team **are** strong." →  "The team **is** strong." (preferred in exams)
- **Why:** Collective noun as one unit = singular.

## Trap 8: Quantifiers (some, most, all)
-  "Some of the water **are** clean." →  "Some of the water **is** clean."
- **Why:** 'water' is uncountable (singular).

# Practice Questions (SSC/Banking Level)

## Set 1: Identify the Error

1. One of the reasons for the delay **are** bad weather.  - **Error:** "are" should be "**is**" (subject: one)

2. Each of the participants **have** received a certificate.  - **Error:** "have" should be "**has**" (subject: each)

3. The team of engineers **are** working on the project.  - **Error:** "are" should be "**is**" (subject: team)

4. There **is** several books on the table.  - **Error:** "is" should be "**are**" (subject: several books)

5. Either Ravi or his brothers **is** coming to the party.  - **Error:** "is" should be "**are**" (nearest subject: brothers)

6. The price of vegetables **have** increased.  - **Error:** "have" should be "**has**" (subject: price)

7. Ten kilometers **are** a long distance for a child.  - **Error:** "are" should be "**is**" (total distance as one unit)

8. Mathematics **are** my favorite subject.  - **Error:** "are" should be "**is**" (subject name is singular)

9. Everyone in the class **are** present today.  - **Error:** "are" should be "**is**" (subject: everyone - singular)

10. The list of candidates **were** published yesterday.  - **Error:** "were" should be "**was**" (subject: list)

## Set 2: Choose the Correct Verb

11. Neither of the answers (is/are) correct.  - **Answer:** **is** (neither = singular)

12. Both of my parents (work/works) in a hospital.  - **Answer:** **work** (both = plural)

13. The government (has/have) announced new policies.  - **Answer:** **has** (collective noun as one unit)

14. Five thousand rupees (is/are) too much for this shirt.  - **Answer:** **is** (total amount = singular)

15. Some of the students (was/were) absent yesterday.  - **Answer:** **were** (students = countable plural)

# Memory Techniques

### . The "Cover Test" for "One of the + Plural"
- **Sentence:** "One of the students are..."
- **Cover:** ~~of the students~~
- **Remains:** "One are..." → Sounds wrong!
- **Correct:** "One **is**..."

### . The "Replace Test" for Collective Nouns
- **Sentence:** "The team is/are winning."
- **Replace with pronoun:** Can you say "**It** is winning"? → Yes! Use **is** (singular)
- **Or:** Do you need "**They** are winning"? → Then use **are** (plural)

### . The "Ignore Middle Words" Technique
- Draw brackets around prepositional phrases:
- "The box [of chocolates] **is** on the table."
- Only the subject (box) and verb (is) remain → Easy to check agreement!

### . The "Sound Check" for Questions
- **Question:** "There is books?"
- **Flip:** "Books is there?" → Sounds wrong!
- **Correct:** "There **are** books."

### . Indefinite Pronoun Memory Trick
- If it has **-one, -body, -thing** → Singular!
- very**one**, some**body**, every**thing** → All take singular verbs (is, has, does)

# Exam Strategy

## For Error Spotting (SSC/Banking):
1. **Scan for these high-probability errors:**  - "One of the + plural noun" with wrong verb (80% error rate)  - Indefinite pronouns (each, everyone) with plural verb (70% error rate)  - Prepositional phrases confusing subject (60% error rate)  - Collective nouns with wrong verb (50% error rate)

2. **Time-saving tip:** In Error Spotting, Subject-Verb Agreement = 80% of grammar errors. Check this FIRST before looking at other grammar rules.

3. **Mark these keywords immediately:**  - **Red flags:** "one of the", "each of", "everyone", "neither", "either...or"  - **Check carefully:** collective nouns (team, family, government), quantifiers (some, most, all), distance/time/money

## For Sentence Correction:
1. Identify the subject (cross out prepositional phrases)
2. Check if subject is singular or plural
3. Match verb accordingly
4. Verify with "sound check" - does it sound natural?

## For Fill in the Blanks:
1. Find the subject first (it may come after the blank)
2. Determine singular/plural
3. Choose verb form accordingly

# Key Takeaways

1. **Golden Rule:** Singular subject = singular verb, Plural subject = plural verb
2. **Most Tested:** "One of the + plural noun" takes SINGULAR verb (is, has, was)
3. **Always Singular:** each, every, everyone, everybody, someone, anyone, no one, neither, either
4. **Always Plural:** both, many, several, few
5. **Ignore Middle Words:** Subject + [prepositional phrase] → match verb with SUBJECT only
6. **Distance/Time/Money:** As total amount = SINGULAR verb
7. **Either...or / Neither...nor:** Verb matches the NEAREST subject
8. **There/Here:** Find the subject AFTER the verb, then match
9. **Collective Nouns:** Usually SINGULAR in Indian exams (team is, government has)
10. **Practice = Perfection:** This topic is 80% of Error Spotting. Master it for guaranteed marks!
