# What is Parts of Speech?

Parts of speech are the building blocks of English language. Every word in English belongs to one of eight categories based on its function in a sentence. Understanding parts of speech is like learning the alphabet before reading - it's the foundation of all grammar.

Think of parts of speech as different types of bricks in a building. Each brick (word) has a specific purpose - some are structural (nouns, verbs), some are decorative (adjectives, adverbs), and some hold everything together (prepositions, conjunctions). When you understand which brick to use where, you can build perfect sentences.

For Indian students preparing for competitive exams, mastering parts of speech is crucial. Whether you're taking SSC, Banking, IELTS, or any other exam, almost every grammar question tests your knowledge of parts of speech - either directly or indirectly.

# Core Concepts

### Nouns (Person, Place, Thing, Idea)

**Definition:** A noun is a word that names a person, place, thing, or idea.

**Rules:**
1. **Common Nouns** name general items: book, city, teacher, country
2. **Proper Nouns** name specific items and are capitalized: Rahul, Mumbai, India, RBI
3. **Countable Nouns** can be counted: one pen, two pens, three pens
4. **Uncountable Nouns** cannot be counted: water, information, furniture

**Examples:**
-  CORRECT: "The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) announced new policies."
-  INCORRECT: "The reserve bank of india announced new policies." → WHY: Proper nouns must be capitalized
-  CORRECT: "I need some information about the UPSC exam."
-  INCORRECT: "I need some informations about the UPSC exam." → WHY: Information is uncountable (no plural form)

---

### Pronouns (I, you, he, she, it, they)

**Definition:** A pronoun is a word that replaces a noun to avoid repetition.

**Rules:**
1. **Subject Pronouns** (I, you, he, she, it, we, they) perform the action
2. **Object Pronouns** (me, you, him, her, it, us, them) receive the action
3. **Possessive Pronouns** (mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs) show ownership

**Examples:**
-  CORRECT: "Priya works at ICICI Bank. She is a manager."
-  INCORRECT: "Priya works at ICICI Bank. Priya is a manager." → WHY: Repetitive; use "She" instead
-  CORRECT: "The book is mine, not yours."
-  INCORRECT: "The book is my, not your." → WHY: After "is", use possessive pronoun, not possessive adjective

---

### Verbs (Action Words and State Verbs)

**Definition:** A verb is a word that shows action or state of being.

**Rules:**
1. **Action Verbs** show what someone does: run, write, study, eat
2. **State Verbs** show conditions or feelings: know, believe, love, seem
3. **Auxiliary Verbs** help main verbs: is, am, are, have, has, will, can

**Examples:**
-  CORRECT: "Amit runs 5 kilometers every morning."
-  INCORRECT: "Amit running 5 kilometers every morning." → WHY: Missing auxiliary verb "is"
-  CORRECT: "I know the answer." (state verb)
-  INCORRECT: "I am knowing the answer." → WHY: State verbs are NOT used in continuous tense

---

### Adjectives (Describing Words)

**Definition:** An adjective is a word that describes or modifies a noun.

**Rules:**
1. Adjectives tell **what kind** (beautiful, difficult), **which one** (this, that), or **how many** (three, several)
2. Adjectives usually come **before** the noun: a beautiful house
3. Adjectives can come **after** linking verbs: The house is beautiful

**Examples:**
-  CORRECT: "Delhi University is a prestigious institution."
-  INCORRECT: "Delhi University is a prestigiously institution." → WHY: Use adjective (prestigious), not adverb (prestigiously)
-  CORRECT: "She bought three expensive books."
-  INCORRECT: "She bought expensive three books." → WHY: Order = quantity (three) before quality (expensive)

---

### Adverbs (Describing Actions)

**Definition:** An adverb is a word that describes or modifies a verb, adjective, or another adverb.

**Rules:**
1. Adverbs tell **how** (quickly, carefully), **when** (now, yesterday), **where** (here, everywhere), or **how much** (very, extremely)
2. Many adverbs end in **-ly**: quick → quickly, careful → carefully
3. Adverbs of manner usually come **after** the verb: She speaks fluently

**Examples:**
-  CORRECT: "He solved the problem quickly."
-  INCORRECT: "He solved the problem quick." → WHY: Use adverb (quickly) to describe verb (solved)
-  CORRECT: "She is extremely intelligent."
-  INCORRECT: "She is extreme intelligent." → WHY: Use adverb (extremely) to describe adjective (intelligent)

---

### Prepositions (in, on, at, by, with)

**Definition:** A preposition is a word that shows the relationship between a noun/pronoun and other words in the sentence.

**Rules:**
1. **Prepositions of Time**: in (months, years), on (days, dates), at (specific times)
2. **Prepositions of Place**: in (inside), on (surface), at (specific location)
3. **Prepositions of Direction**: to, from, into, towards

**Examples:**
-  CORRECT: "The meeting is on Monday at 10 AM in the conference room."
-  INCORRECT: "The meeting is in Monday on 10 AM at the conference room." → WHY: Use "on" for days, "at" for times, "in" for enclosed spaces
-  CORRECT: "I am going to Bangalore by train."
-  INCORRECT: "I am going to Bangalore with train." → WHY: Use "by" for mode of transport (not "with")

---

### Conjunctions (and, but, or, because)

**Definition:** A conjunction is a word that joins words, phrases, or clauses.

**Rules:**
1. **Coordinating Conjunctions** (and, but, or, so, yet) join equal elements
2. **Subordinating Conjunctions** (because, although, if, when, since) join dependent clauses to independent clauses

**Examples:**
-  CORRECT: "Rahul studied hard, so he passed the exam."
-  INCORRECT: "Rahul studied hard, he passed the exam." → WHY: Missing conjunction to connect two complete sentences
-  CORRECT: "She will succeed because she works hard."
-  INCORRECT: "She will succeed, because of she works hard." → WHY: Use "because" (conjunction), not "because of" (preposition + of)

---

### Interjections (Wow!, Ouch!, Hey!)

**Definition:** An interjection is a word that expresses strong emotion or sudden feeling.

**Rules:**
1. Interjections are followed by an **exclamation mark (!)** or **comma (,)**
2. They are NOT grammatically connected to the rest of the sentence
3. Common interjections: Oh!, Wow!, Alas!, Hurray!, Oops!

**Examples:**
-  CORRECT: "Wow! You scored 95% in the exam."
-  INCORRECT: "Wow. You scored 95% in the exam." → WHY: Use exclamation mark (!), not period (.)
-  CORRECT: "Alas! He failed the interview."
-  INCORRECT: "Alas He failed the interview." → WHY: Missing punctuation after interjection

# Practice Problems

### Beginner Level (30%)

1. **Identify the noun in this sentence:** "Mumbai is the financial capital of India."  **Answer:** Mumbai, capital, India - All three are nouns. Mumbai and India are proper nouns (capitalized), capital is a common noun.

2. **Choose the correct pronoun:** "Priya and ___ are going to Delhi." (I / me)  **Answer:** I - Subject pronoun needed (Priya and I are performing the action of going)

### Intermediate Level (50%)

3. **Fill in the blank with correct preposition:** "The train arrived ___ platform 5 ___ 3 PM." (in, on, at)  **Answer:** at, at - "arrived at platform 5 at 3 PM" (use 'at' for specific location and specific time)

4. **Identify the part of speech of the underlined word:** "The students work **hard** to pass the exam."  **Answer:** Adverb - "hard" modifies the verb "work" (tells HOW they work)

5. **Choose the correct word:** "She speaks English very ___." (fluent / fluently)  **Answer:** fluently - Need adverb to modify verb "speaks" (adjective = fluent, adverb = fluently)

### Advanced Level (20%)

6. **Error spotting:** "The informations provided by the RBI is very useful for bankers."  **Answer:** Error in "informations" → Should be "information" (uncountable noun, no plural form)

7. **Identify all parts of speech:** "Oh! The brilliant student quickly solved the difficult problem."  **Answer:**  - Oh = Interjection  - The = Article (Determiner)  - brilliant = Adjective  - student = Noun  - quickly = Adverb  - solved = Verb  - the = Article  - difficult = Adjective  - problem = Noun
