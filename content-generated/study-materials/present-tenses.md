**Level:** A2 | **Time:** 40 mins | **Category:** Grammar

# What is Present Tenses?

Present tenses in English are NOT just about "present time" - this is the biggest confusion for Indian students. Present tenses are about how we view time: is it a habit? happening now? completed with current relevance? or started in past and continuing?

English has FOUR present tenses, each with a specific purpose:
1. **Present Simple** - Habits, facts, routines
2. **Present Continuous** - Actions happening now or around now
3. **Present Perfect** - Completed actions with present relevance
4. **Present Perfect Continuous** - Actions that started in past and are still continuing

Think of present tenses as different camera shots. Present Simple is a still photo showing what happens regularly. Present Continuous is a live video of what's happening now. Present Perfect is a photo album showing "life so far". Present Perfect Continuous is a documentary showing an ongoing story.

For SSC, Banking, and IELTS exams, mastering present tenses is CRITICAL. About 30-40% of all tense-related questions test present tenses, especially Present Perfect vs Past Simple distinction.

# Core Concepts

### . Present Simple Tense (I work, She works)

**Definition:** Used for habits, routines, facts, and general truths.

**Rules:**
1. **Form:** Subject + Base Verb (add -s/-es for he/she/it)  - I/You/We/They work  - He/She/It works
2. **Negative:** Subject + do/does + not + base verb  - I don't work, He doesn't work
3. **Question:** Do/Does + subject + base verb?  - Do you work? Does she work?

**Examples:**
-  CORRECT: "The Reserve Bank of India controls monetary policy." (fact)
-  INCORRECT: "The Reserve Bank of India controlling monetary policy." → WHY: For facts, use Present Simple, not Continuous
-  CORRECT: "Rahul works at HDFC Bank." (routine/permanent situation)
-  INCORRECT: "Rahul is working at HDFC Bank." → WHY: If it's his permanent job, use Simple, not Continuous
-  CORRECT: "The sun rises in the east." (universal truth)
-  INCORRECT: "The sun is rising in the east." → WHY: Universal truths always use Present Simple

**Time Expressions:** always, usually, often, sometimes, never, every day/week/month, on Mondays, in the morning

---

### . Present Continuous Tense (I am working)

**Definition:** Used for actions happening NOW or around NOW, and for temporary situations.

**Rules:**
1. **Form:** Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing  - I am working  - He/She/It is working  - We/You/They are working
2. **Negative:** Subject + am/is/are + not + verb-ing
3. **Question:** Am/Is/Are + subject + verb-ing?

**Examples:**
-  CORRECT: "I am studying for the UPSC exam right now." (happening at this moment)
-  INCORRECT: "I study for the UPSC exam right now." → WHY: "right now" = Present Continuous
-  CORRECT: "She is staying with her aunt in Delhi this month." (temporary situation)
-  INCORRECT: "She stays with her aunt in Delhi this month." → WHY: Temporary situation (this month) needs Continuous
-  CORRECT: "Look! The Prime Minister is arriving at the airport." (happening now)
-  INCORRECT: "Look! The Prime Minister arrives at the airport." → WHY: "Look!" indicates something is happening NOW

**Time Expressions:** now, right now, at the moment, currently, today, this week/month/year, these days

**CRITICAL RULE:** State verbs are NOT used in continuous tense:
-  WRONG: "I am knowing the answer"
-  RIGHT: "I know the answer"

State verbs: know, understand, believe, want, need, like, love, hate, remember, forget, seem, belong, own, have (possession)

---

### . Present Perfect Tense (I have worked)

**Definition:** Used for completed actions that have a connection to the present. Focus is on the RESULT, not when it happened.

**Rules:**
1. **Form:** Subject + have/has + past participle (3rd form)  - I/You/We/They have worked  - He/She/It has worked
2. **Negative:** Subject + have/has + not + past participle
3. **Question:** Have/Has + subject + past participle?

**Examples:**
-  CORRECT: "I have completed the project." (it's finished, result is important)
-  INCORRECT: "I completed the project." → WHY: If the completion is relevant now (you can submit it), use Present Perfect
-  CORRECT: "She has visited Mumbai three times." (life experience, no specific time mentioned)
-  INCORRECT: "She visited Mumbai three times." → WHY: For life experiences without specific time, use Present Perfect
-  CORRECT: "The government has announced new policies." (recent news with current relevance)
-  INCORRECT: "The government announced new policies." → WHY: Recent announcements affecting present situation use Present Perfect

**Time Expressions:** just, already, yet, ever, never, recently, lately, so far, until now, up to now, since, for

**KEY DISTINCTION - Present Perfect vs Past Simple:**
-  "I have lost my pen." (I don't have it now - present result)
-  "I lost my pen yesterday." (specific past time mentioned = Past Simple)
-  "I have lost my pen yesterday." → NEVER use Present Perfect with specific past time

---

### . Present Perfect Continuous (I have been working)

**Definition:** Used for actions that started in the past and are STILL CONTINUING, or just stopped with present evidence.

**Rules:**
1. **Form:** Subject + have/has + been + verb-ing  - I/You/We/They have been working  - He/She/It has been working
2. Focus is on the DURATION and CONTINUITY of the action

**Examples:**
-  CORRECT: "I have been preparing for the SSC exam for six months." (started 6 months ago, still continuing)
-  INCORRECT: "I prepare for the SSC exam for six months." → WHY: Duration from past to now = Present Perfect Continuous
-  CORRECT: "It has been raining since morning." (started in the morning, still raining OR just stopped)
-  INCORRECT: "It is raining since morning." → WHY: "since morning" indicates duration from past = Present Perfect Continuous
-  CORRECT: "Why are your clothes wet? I have been playing cricket." (evidence in present of past action)
-  INCORRECT: "Why are your clothes wet? I played cricket." → WHY: Present evidence needs Present Perfect Continuous

**Time Expressions:** for (duration), since (starting point), all day/week/month, how long

### . Time Expressions for Each Tense

| Tense | Common Time Words | Example |
|-------|-------------------|---------|
| **Present Simple** | always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never, every day/week, on Mondays | "I usually wake up at 6 AM." |
| **Present Continuous** | now, right now, at the moment, currently, today, this week, these days | "I am reading a book right now." |
| **Present Perfect** | just, already, yet, ever, never, recently, lately, so far, still, since, for | "I have just finished my homework." |
| **Present Perfect Continuous** | for, since, all day/week/month, how long, recently | "I have been living in Delhi for 5 years." |

---

### . Present Simple vs Present Continuous

**Permanent situations → Present Simple**
-  "I work at TCS." (permanent job)

**Temporary situations → Present Continuous**
-  "I am working on a special project this month." (temporary)

**Habits/Routines → Present Simple**
-  "I go to the gym every morning." (habit)

**Actions happening now → Present Continuous**
-  "I am going to the gym now." (happening now)

**General truths → Present Simple**
-  "Water boils at 100°C." (fact)

---

### . Present Perfect vs Past Simple (MOST TESTED!)

This is THE most confusing distinction for Indian students because Hindi doesn't make this difference.

**Present Perfect** = Connection to present, no specific time
-  "I have visited the Taj Mahal." (life experience)
-  "Have you ever been to Kashmir?" (any time in life)

**Past Simple** = Specific time in the past, no connection to present
-  "I visited the Taj Mahal last year." (specific time = last year)
-  "I went to Kashmir in 2024." (specific time = 2024)

**RULE:** If you mention WHEN (yesterday, last week, in 2020, two days ago) → use Past Simple, NOT Present Perfect

# Common Mistakes (Indian Students)

| Mistake | Why It Happens | Correct Form |
|---------|----------------|--------------|
| "I am knowing Hindi" | Hindi: मैं जानता हूँ (continuous feel) | "I know Hindi" (state verbs NOT in continuous) |
| "She is having a car" | Hindi: उसके पास कार है (literal translation) | "She has a car" (have = possession, not continuous) |
| "Since two years, I am working here" | Hindi word order: दो साल से, मैं यहाँ काम कर रहा हूँ | "I have been working here for two years" (Present Perfect Continuous with FOR, not SINCE + number) |
| "I have gone there yesterday" | Confusion between Present Perfect and Past Simple | "I went there yesterday" (specific time = Past Simple) |
| "He is going to office daily" | Using Continuous for routine | "He goes to office daily" (routine = Simple, not Continuous) |
| "I am living in Delhi since 2020" | Hindi: मैं 2020 से दिल्ली में रह रहा हूँ | "I have been living in Delhi since 2020" (duration from past = Perfect Continuous) |

# Practice Problems

## Beginner Level (30%)

1. **Choose the correct form:** "The sun ___ in the east." (rise / rises / is rising)  **Answer:** rises - Universal truth = Present Simple, 3rd person singular adds -s

2. **Fill in the blank:** "Look! The children ___ in the park." (play / are playing / have played)  **Answer:** are playing - "Look!" = happening now = Present Continuous

## Intermediate Level (50%)

3. **Present Simple or Continuous?** "I usually ___ (drink) coffee, but today I ___ (drink) tea."  **Answer:** drink (habit = Simple), am drinking (today = temporary, happening now = Continuous)

4. **Present Perfect or Past Simple?** "I ___ (visit) the Red Fort many times, but I ___ (visit) it last week with my family."  **Answer:** have visited (life experience, no specific time = Present Perfect), visited (last week = specific time = Past Simple)

5. **Error Spotting:** "Since three months, she is preparing for IELTS."  **Answer:** Error: "Since three months" and "is preparing" → Correct: "For three months, she has been preparing for IELTS" (duration = FOR not SINCE with numbers, and Present Perfect Continuous)

## Advanced Level (20%)

6. **Choose all correct sentences:**  A) "I know him for five years."  B) "I have known him for five years."  C) "I am knowing him for five years."  D) "I have been knowing him for five years."  **Answer:** Only B is correct  - A is wrong: duration from past needs Present Perfect ("have known")  - C is wrong: "know" is a state verb, not used in continuous  - D is wrong: "know" is a state verb, Present Perfect Continuous not used

7. **Complete the conversation:**  A: "Where is Rahul?"  B: "He ___ (go) to the market. He ___ (be) back in 10 minutes."  **Answer:** has gone, will be  - "has gone" = Present Perfect (he went and is not here now)  - "will be" = Future Simple
