## 📐 Lesson Structure Framework (STRICT - Must Follow)

Every lesson MUST have exactly these 5 sections:

### 1. Title & Micro-Goal
**Format:**
```
## [Clear, Action-Oriented Title]

**What You'll Learn:** [1-sentence explanation of what user will be able to say/do after this 5-minute lesson]
```

**Example:**
```
## "I Am, You Are, They Are" - Describing Yourself and Others

**What You'll Learn:** By the end of this lesson, you'll be able to introduce yourself, describe how you feel, and talk about other people using am, is, and are.
```

**Rules:**
- Title in quotes if it's a phrase pattern
- Keep micro-goal under 25 words
- Focus on OUTPUT (what they can say), not INPUT (what they'll study)

---

### 2. The Pattern Block (The "Formula")

**Format:**
```
### The Pattern

[Subject] + am/is/are + [Adjective/Noun/Location]

**Examples:**
I + am + tired.
You + are + a teacher.
They + are + at home.
```

**Visual Format:**
Use clean Markdown boxes with brackets `[replaceable]` and fixed words in plain text.

**Rules:**
- Maximum 3 lines in the pattern
- Use brackets for variable parts: `[Subject]`, `[Action Word]`, `[Time]`
- Show 3 concrete examples applying the pattern
- Keep it VISUAL - learner should see the structure instantly

**Good Pattern Block:**
```
### The Pattern

[I/You/We/They] + don't + [Action Word]
[He/She/It] + doesn't + [Action Word]

**Examples:**
→ I don't like coffee.
→ She doesn't eat meat.
→ They don't speak Spanish.
```

**Bad Pattern Block (too complex):**
```
Negative form: Subject + auxiliary verb (do/does) + not + base form of main verb
```

---

### 3. Real-World Audio/Text Prompts (5 Examples)

**Format:**
```
### When to Use This

**Example 1: [Context]**
"[Natural sentence]"
→ *[Brief note explaining when/why to say this]*

**Example 2: [Context]**
"[Natural sentence]"
→ *[Brief note explaining when/why to say this]*

[... 5 total examples]
```

**Example:**
```
### When to Use This

**Example 1: Introducing Yourself**
"Hi, I'm Rahul. I am a software engineer."
→ *Use "I am" when telling someone your name or job*

**Example 2: Describing Your Feelings**
"I am tired today. I need some rest."
→ *Use "I am" + feeling word to talk about how you feel right now*

**Example 3: Talking About Location**
"My parents are in Mumbai. They are visiting my sister."
→ *Use "are" for plural subjects like "my parents," "they," "we"*

**Example 4: Describing Others**
"She is a doctor. She is very kind."
→ *Use "is" for he/she/it (singular)*

**Example 5: At the Office**
"We are ready for the meeting. Are you ready?"
→ *Use "are" for "we/you/they" and also in questions*
```

**Rules:**
- MUST be hyper-practical, everyday situations
- Include Indian context where relevant (names, places, situations)
- Show WHEN to use the pattern, not just HOW
- Keep explanations under 15 words
- Use → arrow for the explanation note
- Use *italics* for the note

---

### 4. The "Watch Out!" Rule (The Pivot Point)

**Format:**
```
### Watch Out! Common Mistake

❌ **Wrong:** [Incorrect sentence]
✅ **Right:** [Correct sentence]

**Why:** [2-3 sentence explanation of why learners make this mistake and how to fix it]
```

**Example:**
```
### Watch Out! Common Mistake

❌ **Wrong:** I am engineer.
✅ **Right:** I am an engineer.

**Why:** In English, you need "a" or "an" before jobs/professions. Use "an" before words starting with vowel sounds (a, e, i, o, u). Hindi speakers often forget this because Hindi doesn't use articles. Always say: "I am A doctor," "She is AN artist."
```

**Rules:**
- Focus on ONE mistake per lesson (the most common one)
- Explain WHY learners make this mistake (often L1 interference)
- Keep explanation under 50 words
- Use ❌ and ✅ symbols
- Address Indian learners specifically when relevant

---

### 5. Immediate Micro-Quiz (3 Items)

**Format:**
```
### Quick Check: Can You Do It?

**Question 1: Fill in the Blank**
[Question with blank]
a) Option A
b) Option B
c) Option C

**Question 2: Sentence Scramble**
Put these words in the correct order:
[words / to / arrange]

**Question 3: Real-World Choice**
[Situation description]
What do you say?
a) Option A
b) Option B
c) Option C

---

### Answers & Explanations

**Answer 1:** [Letter] - [1-sentence explanation]

**Answer 2:** [Correct sentence] - [1-sentence explanation]

**Answer 3:** [Letter] - [1-sentence explanation]
```

**Example:**
```
### Quick Check: Can You Do It?

**Question 1: Fill in the Blank**
My brother _____ a teacher.
a) am
b) is
c) are

**Question 2: Sentence Scramble**
Put these words in the correct order:
ready / We / the / for / are / exam

**Question 3: Real-World Choice**
Your friend asks: "How are you today?"
What do you say?
a) I very good.
b) I am very good.
c) I am very well.

---

### Answers & Explanations

**Answer 1:** b) is - Use "is" with he/she/it (singular). "My brother" = he (singular).

**Answer 2:** We are ready for the exam. - Pattern: [We] + are + [adjective] + for + [thing].

**Answer 3:** c) I am very well. - Use "well" for health/feelings, not "good." Always include "am."
```

**Rules:**
- Question 1: MUST be fill-in-the-blank (tests pattern recognition)
- Question 2: MUST be sentence scramble (tests word order)
- Question 3: MUST be real-world choice (tests practical application)
- Provide 3 options for MCQ questions
- Answers at the END (not after each question)
- Keep explanations under 20 words each
