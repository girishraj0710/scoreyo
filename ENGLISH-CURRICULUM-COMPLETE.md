### **MODULE 3: Tenses Mastery** (A1-A2)
*All 12 tenses with rules, examples, and practice*

#### 3.1 Simple Present Tense (100 questions)
- **Structure:** Subject + V1(s/es)
- **Usage:** Daily routines, habits, facts, universal truths
- He **plays** cricket every day
- The sun **rises** in the east
- Adding -s/-es for he/she/it
- Negative: don't/doesn't + V1
- Questions: Do/Does + subject + V1?
- **Time expressions:** always, usually, often, sometimes, never, every day

#### 3.2 Present Continuous Tense (100 questions)
- **Structure:** Subject + is/am/are + V-ing
- **Usage:** Actions happening right now, temporary situations
- I **am studying** English now
- She **is cooking** dinner
- Adding -ing to verbs (play→playing, run→running, write→writing)
- Negative: is/am/are + not + V-ing
- Questions: Is/Am/Are + subject + V-ing?
- **Time expressions:** now, at the moment, right now, currently
- **Stative Verbs** NOT used in continuous (know, love, understand, believe)

#### 3.3 Present Perfect Tense (120 questions)
- **Structure:** Subject + has/have + V3
- **Usage:** Past actions with present relevance, experience, recently completed
- I **have finished** my homework
- She **has visited** the Taj Mahal
- Regular V3: -ed (walked, played, studied)
- Irregular V3: (eat→eaten, go→gone, write→written, see→seen)
- Negative: has/have + not + V3
- Questions: Has/Have + subject + V3?
- **Time expressions:** ever, never, already, yet, just, recently, so far, since, for
- **Present Perfect vs Simple Past**

#### 3.4 Simple Past Tense (100 questions)
- **Structure:** Subject + V2
- **Usage:** Completed actions at a specific past time
- I **went** to Mumbai yesterday
- She **studied** English last year
- Regular V2: -ed (walked, played, studied)
- Irregular V2: (go→went, eat→ate, see→saw, write→wrote)
- Negative: didn't + V1
- Questions: Did + subject + V1?
- **Time expressions:** yesterday, last week/month/year, ago, in 1999

#### 3.5 Past Continuous Tense (100 questions)
- **Structure:** Subject + was/were + V-ing
- **Usage:** Action in progress at a specific past time
- I **was studying** at 8 PM yesterday
- They **were playing** when it started raining
- Two simultaneous actions (while, when)
- Interrupted actions
- **Time expressions:** at 5pm yesterday, while, when, as

#### 3.6 Simple Future Tense (100 questions)
- **Structure:** Subject + will + V1
- **Usage:** Future predictions, spontaneous decisions, promises
- It **will rain** tomorrow
- I **will help** you
- Negative: will not (won't) + V1
- Questions: Will + subject + V1?
- **Time expressions:** tomorrow, next week/month/year, soon, later
- **Will vs Be going to**

#### 3.7 Future Continuous (80 questions)
- **Structure:** Subject + will be + V-ing
- I **will be studying** at this time tomorrow

#### 3.8 Present Perfect Continuous (80 questions)
- **Structure:** Subject + has/have been + V-ing
- I **have been studying** for 2 hours

#### 3.9 Past Perfect (80 questions)
- **Structure:** Subject + had + V3
- She **had left** before I arrived

#### 3.10 Past Perfect Continuous (60 questions)
- **Structure:** Subject + had been + V-ing

#### 3.11 Future Perfect (60 questions)
- **Structure:** Subject + will have + V3

#### 3.12 Tense Comparison & Usage (150 questions)
- Timeline of all 12 tenses
- When to use which tense
- Common confusions solved
- Mixed tense practice

**Module 3 Total: 1,130 questions**

### **MODULE 4: Sentence Construction** (A2-B1)
*Build correct English sentences like a native*

#### 4.1 Types of Sentences (100 questions)
- **Simple Sentence:** One independent clause
  - I like pizza.
- **Compound Sentence:** Two independent clauses
  - I like pizza, but she likes pasta.
- **Complex Sentence:** Independent + dependent clause
  - I like pizza because it tastes good.
- **Compound-Complex:** Multiple independent + dependent
- Using Conjunctions (and, but, or, so, because, although, while)

#### 4.2 Subject-Verb Agreement (100 questions)
- Singular subject → singular verb (He **plays**)
- Plural subject → plural verb (They **play**)
- Compound subjects (Ram **and** Shyam **are** friends)
- Collective nouns (The team **is** winning)
- Indefinite pronouns (Everyone **is** here)
- Either/Neither rules
- Tricky cases (news, mathematics, economics)

#### 4.3 Active & Passive Voice (120 questions)
- **Active:** Subject performs action (I wrote a letter)
- **Passive:** Subject receives action (A letter was written by me)
- When to use passive
- Passive in all tenses
- Active ↔ Passive conversion
- By agent (when to include/omit)

#### 4.4 Direct & Indirect Speech (120 questions)
- Direct: He said, "I am happy"
- Indirect: He said that he was happy
- Tense changes in reported speech
- Pronoun changes (I→he, my→his)
- Time/place changes (today→that day, here→there)
- Reporting questions
- Reporting commands
